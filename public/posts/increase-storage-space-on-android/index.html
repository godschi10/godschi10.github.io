    How to Increase Storage Space on Android (9 Easy Ways to Free Up Memory)

    October 20, 2023 · 12 min

    Running out of storage on your Android phone can be incredibly frustrating. One moment you're trying to install a new app or take a photo, and the next you see the dreaded “Storage Almost Full” warning. When this happens, your phone may slow down, apps may crash, and even basic tasks become difficult.

    The good news is that you don’t need to buy a new phone to fix this. With a few simple steps, you can free up space on Android, clear unnecessary files, and get rid of the “Android storage full” problem in minutes.

    Quick Summary: How to Free Up Space Fast

    • Clear cached files: Go to Settings → Storage → Cached Data, then remove temporary files that apps no longer need.
    • Delete large files and old downloads: Open Files / My Files → Downloads and remove videos, APKs, and large documents you no longer use.
    • Empty the trash in apps like Google Photos: Deleted photos remain in the Trash/Bin for 30 days unless you permanently delete them.

    1. Use the Files by Google App (The Fastest Method)

    If your Android phone is showing an Android storage full warning, the fastest solution is using the Files by Google app. This is Google’s official file management tool, and it’s designed specifically to help you free up space on Android safely.

    Most Android phones already have it pre-installed. If not, you can download Files by Google from the Play Store. The app automatically scans your phone and suggests files you can safely remove, making it one of the easiest ways to delete junk files on Android.

    Steps to Free Up Space Using Files by Google

    1. Open Files by Google.
    2. Tap the Clean tab at the bottom of the screen.
    3. Review suggestions like Junk files, Large files, and Duplicate files.
    4. Tap Free up next to the category you want to clean.

    The app will safely remove temporary files, unused app data, and other clutter that builds up over time. This can instantly clear Android memory and recover several hundred megabytes, or even gigabytes of storage.

    2. Back Up and Delete Photos/Videos

    Photos and videos are usually the biggest storage hogs on Android phones. Just a few 4K videos or hundreds of photos can quickly trigger the Android storage full warning.

    The easiest way to fix this is by backing up your media to Google Photos and then removing the local copies from your device.

    Steps to Free Up Space Using Google Photos

    1. Open Google Photos.
    2. Tap your profile picture in the top-right corner.
    3. Tap Free up space.
    4. Confirm when the app asks to remove backed-up photos and videos.

    Google Photos will automatically delete the files stored on your phone while keeping them safely backed up in your Google account. This means you can still view, download, or share them anytime from the cloud.

    This method can instantly free up several gigabytes of space, especially if your phone has months or years of photos stored locally.

    3. Clear App Cache and Data

    Apps store temporary files on your phone to load faster. Over time, these files pile up and take up valuable storage. Clearing them is one of the easiest ways to free up space on Android without deleting your apps.

    Here’s the difference:

    • Cache: Temporary files and junk data apps store for speed. Safe to delete.
    • Data: Your login info, settings, and saved preferences. Deleting this resets the app.

    Removing cached files helps clear Android memory and can fix slow or buggy apps at the same time.

    Steps to Clear App Cache

    1. Open Settings.
    2. Tap Apps.
    3. Tap See all apps.
    4. Sort the apps by Size to find the biggest ones.
    5. Tap the app you want to clean.
    6. Tap Storage & cache.
    7. Tap Clear cache.

    Repeat this for apps that tend to store large caches, such as browsers, social media apps, or streaming apps.

    ⚠️ Pro Tip:
    Clearing Cache is safe and won’t log you out of the app. However, tapping Clear Data will reset the app completely and remove your login details, saved settings, and offline files. Only use this option if you’re okay signing in again.

    4. Uninstall Unused and Pre-installed Apps (Bloatware)

    Apps can quietly eat up storage space, especially ones you installed once and never used again. If your phone shows Android storage full, take a few minutes to review your apps and remove anything you don’t actually need.

    A simple rule: If you haven’t used the app in the last 3 months, uninstall it. Deleting unused apps can instantly free up space on Android and also reduce background activity that slows your phone down.

    Steps to Uninstall Apps

    1. Open Settings.
    2. Tap Apps.
    3. Tap See all apps.
    4. Tap the app you want to remove.
    5. Tap Uninstall.

    You can also quickly uninstall apps by long-pressing the app icon on your home screen and tapping Uninstall.

    Removing unused apps helps clear Android memory and often frees hundreds of megabytes, or more if the app stores offline content.

    How to Disable Apps You Can't Uninstall

    Some phones come with pre-installed carrier or manufacturer apps, often called bloatware. These apps sometimes cannot be uninstalled, but you can still stop them from running.

    Disabling these apps prevents them from using storage for updates or running in the background.

    Steps to Disable Bloatware Apps

    1. Open Settings.
    2. Tap Apps.
    3. Tap See all apps.
    4. Select the app you want to disable.
    5. Tap Disable.

    Once disabled, the app will no longer run or update, which can help free up space on Android and improve performance.

    5. Clean Up Messaging Apps (WhatsApp, Telegram)

    Messaging apps are one of the biggest hidden reasons for Android storage full errors. Photos, videos, voice notes, and forwarded files from group chats can quietly build up and consume several gigabytes of storage.

    Apps like WhatsApp and Telegram automatically download media unless you change the settings. Cleaning them regularly can quickly free up space on Android.

    How to Clean Up WhatsApp Storage

    WhatsApp has a built-in tool that shows you the largest files and the chats using the most storage.

    Steps:

    1. Open WhatsApp.
    2. Tap the three-dot menu in the top-right corner.
    3. Tap Settings.
    4. Tap Storage and data.
    5. Tap Manage storage.
    6. Review sections like Larger than 5 MB and Forwarded many times.
    7. Select the files you want to remove and tap Delete.

    Deleting large videos and old forwarded media is one of the fastest ways to clear Android memory.

    How to Clear Telegram Cache

    Telegram stores a lot of media locally so chats load faster. Over time, this cache can grow very large.

    Steps:

    1. Open Telegram.
    2. Tap Settings.
    3. Tap Data and Storage.
    4. Tap Storage Usage.
    5. Tap Clear Telegram Cache.
    6. Select which media types to remove and confirm.

    This will delete temporary files stored on your phone while keeping them accessible in the cloud.

    Set Telegram to Auto-Delete Old Media (Highly Recommended)

    To prevent the problem from coming back, you can set Telegram to automatically remove cached media after a certain period.

    Steps:

    1. Open Telegram.
    2. Tap Settings.
    3. Tap Data and Storage.
    4. Tap Storage Usage.
    5. Under Keep Media, choose 3 days, 1 week, or 1 month.

    This feature automatically clears old files so your phone doesn’t run into Android storage full issues again.

    6. Delete Offline Content (Maps, Music, Movies)

    Many people forget about offline downloads they saved for travel, flights, or road trips. Apps like Google Maps, Spotify, Netflix, and YouTube store these files directly on your phone, and they can take up hundreds of megabytes or even several gigabytes.

    If you're trying to free up space on Android, clearing old downloads is a quick win.

    Delete Offline Maps in Google Maps

    Offline maps are useful when traveling without internet, but they can take up a lot of storage.

    Steps:

    1. Open Google Maps.
    2. Tap your profile picture in the top-right corner.
    3. Tap Offline maps.
    4. Select a downloaded map.
    5. Tap Delete.

    Removing maps you no longer need helps increase storage space on Android quickly.

    Remove Downloaded Music in Spotify

    Downloaded playlists allow offline listening, but large playlists can fill your phone fast.

    Steps:

    1. Open Spotify.
    2. Go to Your Library.
    3. Tap Downloaded to see offline content.
    4. Open a downloaded playlist or album.
    5. Toggle Download off to remove it from your device.

    The music will still be available for streaming when you’re online.

    Delete Downloaded Videos in Netflix or YouTube

    Movies and shows are among the largest files stored on Android phones.

    Netflix Steps:

    1. Open Netflix.
    2. Tap Downloads.
    3. Tap the pencil/edit icon.
    4. Select videos you want to remove.
    5. Tap Delete.

    YouTube Steps:

    1. Open YouTube.
    2. Tap Library.
    3. Tap Downloads.
    4. Select a video.
    5. Tap Delete from downloads.

    Removing offline videos can instantly clear Android memory and solve the Android storage full problem.

    7. Empty the Android Trash/Bin

    Many Android users don’t realize that deleted files aren’t always removed immediately. Apps like Photos and file managers keep them in a Trash/Bin folder for about 30 days before permanent deletion.

    That means even after deleting pictures or videos, they may still be taking up storage. Learning how to empty trash on Android can instantly recover valuable space.

    Empty Trash in Google Photos

    When you delete photos or videos in Google Photos, they move to the Trash instead of being permanently removed.

    Steps:

    1. Open Google Photos.
    2. Tap Library at the bottom.
    3. Tap Trash (or Bin).
    4. Tap Select.
    5. Choose the items you want to delete permanently.
    6. Tap Delete permanently.

    You can also tap Empty Trash to remove everything at once.

    Empty Trash in Files by Google

    Files you delete in the Files app also stay in a trash folder temporarily.

    Steps:

    1. Open Files by Google.
    2. Tap the three-line menu (top left).
    3. Tap Trash.
    4. Tap Empty Trash.
    5. Confirm the deletion.

    Once the trash is emptied, the files are permanently removed, which can immediately free up space on Android.

    8. Utilize MicroSD Cards (For Compatible Devices)

    Some Android phones include a microSD card slot, which allows you to expand storage cheaply. If your device supports it, this is one of the easiest long-term solutions when your Android storage is full.

    A microSD card lets you store photos, videos, music, and sometimes apps on external storage instead of your phone’s internal memory.

    Note: Many newer flagship phones no longer include SD card slots, but many Samsung, Motorola, and budget Android devices still support them.

    Move Photos and Videos to the SD Card

    The easiest way to increase storage space on Android is to save new photos directly to the SD card.

    Steps:

    1. Open your Camera app.
    2. Tap Settings.
    3. Find Storage location.
    4. Select SD card.

    New photos and videos will now be stored on the card instead of internal storage.

    Move Apps to the SD Card (If Supported)

    Some apps can also be moved to external storage.

    Steps:

    1. Open Settings.
    2. Tap Apps.
    3. Tap See all apps.
    4. Select an app.
    5. Tap Storage.
    6. Tap Change → SD card.

    Not every app supports this feature, but moving larger apps can help free up space on Android quickly.

    9. Factory Reset (The Nuclear Option)

    If you’ve tried everything and your Android storage is still full, a factory reset can completely clean your device. Over time, phones accumulate hidden files, leftover app data, and system clutter that regular cleaning can’t remove.

    A factory reset wipes the phone and restores it to the same state it was in when you first bought it. This often solves deep Android storage problems, especially on older devices.

    ⚠️ Important Warning:
    A factory reset will erase EVERYTHING on your phone — photos, apps, contacts, files, and settings. Before doing this, make sure you back up your phone using Google Photos, Google Drive, or another backup service.

    Steps to Factory Reset Your Android Phone

    1. Open Settings.
    2. Tap System.
    3. Tap Reset options.
    4. Tap Erase all data (factory reset).
    5. Tap Reset phone and confirm.

    Your phone will restart and begin the reset process. After setup, you can restore your data from your backup.

    A factory reset is the most powerful way to clear Android memory, but it should only be used when other methods fail.

    Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

    Why is my Android storage full when I have nothing on it?

    Even if your phone looks empty, storage can still be used by system files, hidden app cache, and background data from apps like WhatsApp or Telegram. Temporary files, downloaded media, and app updates can silently consume several gigabytes. Clearing cache and reviewing large files is usually the fastest way to free up space on Android.

    Does clearing cache delete pictures?

    No. Clearing cache does not delete your photos, videos, or personal files. Cache only removes temporary files that apps store to load faster. It’s a safe way to clear Android memory without affecting your important data.

    What is "System" storage on Android?

    System storage contains the core files required for Android to operate, including the operating system, security updates, and built-in services. This space cannot be manually deleted because it’s necessary for the phone to function properly. However, system size can increase slightly after software updates.

    How do I check what is taking up space on my Android phone?

    You can easily see what’s using your storage by going to Settings → Storage. This page breaks down usage into categories like Apps, Photos, Videos, Audio, and System. Reviewing this section helps identify the biggest files so you can quickly increase storage space on Android.

    What files should I delete first to free up space on Android?

    Start by deleting large videos, duplicate photos, old downloads, and unused apps. Messaging apps and downloaded media often take up the most space. Using tools like Files by Google can quickly identify large files you can safely remove.

    How often should I clean my Android storage?

    It’s a good idea to review your storage once every 1–2 months. Regularly clearing cache, deleting unused apps, and removing old downloads can prevent the Android storage full warning from appearing again.

    How do I empty trash on Android?

    Many apps keep deleted files in a Trash or Bin folder for up to 30 days. To permanently remove them, open apps like Google Photos or Files by Google, go to Trash, and tap Empty Trash. Doing this can instantly reclaim storage space.

    Conclusion

    Running into the “Android storage full” warning is frustrating, but the good news is that it’s usually easy to fix. By deleting junk files, cleaning messaging apps, removing unused downloads, and occasionally clearing app cache, you can quickly increase storage space on Android without needing a new phone.

    The best long-term habit is simple: schedule regular storage cleanups. For example, set a monthly reminder to clear WhatsApp media, Telegram cache, and old downloads. Small maintenance steps like this prevent clutter from building up and help keep your phone running smoothly.
